    Islam in Oman

    The philosophy of Islam in Oman is based on religious tolerance and avoiding conflict and violence. Other religious views or models of interpretation must be respected. Uniquely, communal prayer to God in Oman knows no theological disputes. Prayers in the mosques throughout the country are conducted with Sunnis and Shiites side by side with Ibadhis, who constitute the dominant sect in Oman.

    Towards United Human Values

    On occasion of UNESCO's "International Day for Tolerance", Sultanate of Oman launched on November 16th 2019, from the Indonesian capital Jakarta, a new global initiative entitled: “Towards united human values” to spread concepts of mutual understanding, religious tolerance and peaceful coexistence, to strengthen human relations through this new initiative around the world.

    Fostering Coexistence

    Today, a large number of adherents of different religions live peacefully in Oman, as the state guarantees the freedom to practice religious rites for both citizens and residents. The Ministry of Endowments and Religious Affairs is legally responsible for the various religious communities in Oman, and provides them with the necessary facilities to practice their religious rites.



    Fostering Coexistence

    For many years the government of Oman has been promoting interfaith dialogue to foster religious tolerance, mutual understanding, and peaceful coexistence on a global scale.
